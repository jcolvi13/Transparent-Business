Problem Statement (Who is the user? What problem or pain point do they experience today?)

The ideal users are independent stock traders that wish to trade ethically and support ethical business practices while still growing their portfolio. The current market environment is toxic and supports monopolies and unsustainable growth. Stock traders may feel conflicted about how they invest their money and what they personally support. Everyone wants their money to grow, so it is also easy to invest in high performing megacorporations without concern for the ethics of a company. 


Why Now? (Why does this problem matter in the next 3–5 years? What changed that makes this possible now?)

As the market continues to grow in favor of megacorporations, people will seek a way to invest without compromising their morals or beliefs. 
Now that there has been a significant development in machine learning, AI can now be implemented for the benefit of the average stock market traders. Previously, this role was filled by private financial advisors and institutions, but these are not always available to the average person or aligned with their individual values, especially for people who are not heavily invested in or familiar with stock market exchanges.


Proposed AI-Powered Solution (What does your product do for the user? Where does AI/ML add unique value vs simple rules / heuristics?)

The product will create a values/ethics profile of the user, aggregate ethical and trading data of corporations, and provide a summary of the corporations financially and ethically.
A major difference between this approach and others is the correlation-based stock replacement concept, which has not been done before. This approach allows investors to invest in markets they have interest in but choose a different type of company that better aligns with their values. Additionally, the confidence score determining whether the research ran by the AI contains sufficient information to make a definitive decision is a transparent, unique take that can lead the company to success, given that there is often a lack of conversation about the confidence that AI has over certain responses it provides. Through machine learning, we are able to keep track of a significant amount of data and condense it so we are capable of making timely, accurate decisions. 


Initial Technical Concept (What data would you need? What model(s) might you use (e.g., GPT-style text model, classifier, recommender, vision model? How could your nanoGPT work feed into this (e.g., custom generative component, fine-tuning, evaluation)?

The general data we would look for is stock market information, including current data and historical trends, company financial statements, political alignments, and macroeconomic data. This provides fact-based information to understand if the money invested is going to produce a profit. ESG ratings, news and media outlets, and social sentiment information instead can provide ethics-based information, both taking into account corporate ethics data and general public statements. 


Scope for MVP (What can you realistically build in ~6 weeks? Define a very concrete v1 feature: “A user can ___ and our system returns ___.”) 

In 6 weeks we can build an AI assistant that aggregates data and provides up to date information of the ethical practices of large corporations and suggests stocks within the same sector that are more ethically aligned with the user within a small set of corporations.
A user can search for a corporation from a set and our system returns trading options and an ethical profile/summary.


Risks and Open Questions (Top 3 unknowns (e.g., data availability, evaluation, user adoption).)

One of the potential risks includes the AI assistant choosing companies that are not profitable or potentially cause the consumer to lose money rather than making a profit. Another risk is the AI being incapable of finding sufficient information to offer a better alternative or providing incorrect information to an investor. A third risk is the potential backlash a company may have if our product provides negative remarks towards investing in their respective company.
